India's Biodiversity

LOOKING FOR:

  1. Andhra Pradesh

    Andhra Pradesh boasts diverse ecosystems including coastal regions, Eastern Ghats, and Deccan Plateau. It is home to rich flora and fauna. The state has a tropical climate, which supports a wide range of biodiversity. It is known for its mangrove forests, lakes, and rivers. Andhra Pradesh is also famous for its bird sanctuaries, such as Pulicat Lake Bird Sanctuary.

    • Krishna Wildlife Sanctuary
    • Papikonda Wildlife Sanctuary

  2. Arunachal Pradesh

    Arunachal Pradesh is known for its pristine forests, alpine meadows, and diverse wildlife. It is a biodiversity hotspot in India. The state is home to several indigenous tribes, and its rich cultural heritage is intertwined with its natural beauty. Arunachal Pradesh has numerous rivers, including the Brahmaputra and its tributaries, which sustain a variety of aquatic life.

    • Namdapha National Park
    • Keibul Lamjao National Park
    • Bugun Liocichla - Hotspot Bird
    • Cirsium vulgare - medicinal herb

  3. Assam

    Assam is characterized by its lush greenery and rich biodiversity. It is home to various wildlife sanctuaries and national parks. The state is famous for its tea gardens and is known as the largest tea-producing region in India. Assam is also known for its rhinoceros population in Kaziranga National Park and its annual cultural festival, Bihu.

    • Kaziranga National Park
    • Manas National Park
    • Borjuli Wild Rice - Rare Species

  4. Bihar

    Bihar is home to various natural habitats including forests, wetlands, and grasslands. It supports a diverse range of plant and animal species. The state has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its ancient history and archaeological sites. Bihar is also famous for its religious tourism, with attractions such as Bodh Gaya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    • Valmiki National Park
    • Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary
    • Barbar Caves

    Chhattisgarh's biodiversity includes dense forests, rivers, and wildlife. It is known for its rich tribal culture and diverse ecosystems. The state is rich in mineral resources and is one of the leading producers of coal in India. Chhattisgarh is also known for its waterfalls, ancient temples, and wildlife sanctuaries like Indravati National Park.

    • Indravati National Park
